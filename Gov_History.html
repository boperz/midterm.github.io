-Tagbilaran Balay Kabilin in Bohol-

Tagbilaran Balay Kabilin in Bohol offers a glimpse into the region's rich heritage through traditional architecture and diverse artifacts, providing an immersive experience for visitors.



-Blood pact-

The 1565 Sandugo pact united Spanish explorer Miguel Lopez de Legazpi and Bohol chieftain Datu Sikatuna, symbolizing their alliance with a blend of wine and blood. This historic treaty, one of the earliest between Filipinos and Spanish colonists, is celebrated annually in Bohol through festivals honoring their shared heritage.

-Carlos P. Garcia Heritage-
-Museum-

The Carlos P. Garcia Heritage Museum in Tagbilaran City, Bohol, honors the 8th President of the Philippines. It displays personal belongings and memorabilia, offering insights into Garcia's life and presidency

-Baclayon Church-

Baclayon Church in Bohol, Philippines, is an ancient Spanish colonial church known for its Baroque architecture and religious significance. Despite damage from natural disasters, including the 2013 earthquake, efforts are ongoing to preserve this cultural treasure.

-Mahogany Forest-

The Tagbilaran Mahogany Forest in Bohol, Philippines, is a lush 864-hectare area known for its dense growth of mahogany trees. Planted in the 1960s for reforestation, it's now a popular spot for eco-tourism, offering hiking trails and opportunities for birdwatching and photography.
