The pact was sealed through a symbolic blood compact ceremony, where both leaders made a small incision in their arms and mixed their blood with wine, signifying friendship, alliance, and mutual respect. This event is considered one of the earliest recorded treaties of friendship between the indigenous Filipinos and the Spanish colonizers. The Sandugo is commemorated annually in Bohol through festivals and events to celebrate the cultural heritage and unity of the people.

-BACLAYON CHURCH-

The Tagbilaran Baclayon Church, officially known as the Church of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception, is a historic church located in Baclayon, Bohol, Philippines. Constructed in the late 16th century by Spanish missionaries, it is one of the oldest stone churches in the country and is considered a significant cultural and religious landmark. The church features a Baroque architectural style with intricate details and is renowned for its hand-carved wooden altars and religious artifacts. Despite facing natural disasters and restoration efforts, the church remains a symbol of faith and heritage in the region, attracting tourists and pilgrims alike.

-Carlos P. Garcia Heritage-
-Museum-

The Tagbilaran Carlos P. Garcia Heritage Museum is a cultural institution located in Tagbilaran City, Bohol, Philippines. Named after the eighth president of the Philippines, Carlos P. Garcia, who hailed from Bohol, the museum showcases artifacts, memorabilia, and exhibits related to his life, presidency, and the history of Bohol. Visitors can explore Garcia's personal belongings, historical documents, photographs, and other items that provide insight into his life and legacy. The museum serves as a valuable resource for preserving and promoting the rich cultural heritage of Bohol and honoring the contributions of Carlos P. Garcia to Philippine history.

-balay kabilin - bohol heritage house and museum-

Tagbilaran Balay Kabilin, located in Tagbilaran City, Bohol, is a captivating heritage house and museum that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural and historical heritage of the region. The museum showcases traditional Filipino architecture and features a collection of artifacts, artworks, and historical items that highlight the diverse heritage of Bohol. Visitors can explore various exhibits, including displays of ancestral homes, antique furniture, traditional clothing, and religious artifacts. Tagbilaran Balay Kabilin provides an immersive experience, allowing visitors to learn about the customs, traditions, and way of life of the people of Bohol throughout the centuries. It serves as an important cultural and educational institution, preserving and promoting the heritage of the region for future generations to appreciate and enjoy.
