About Tagbilaran

Philippines' Tagbilaran City is a bustling metropolis that may be found on the island of Bohol. As the capital of the province, it offers a blend of cultural attractions, historical sites, and contemporary conveniences. The famous St. Joseph the Worker Cathedral and the adjoining Blood Compact Shrine, which honor the first international covenant of brotherhood between the Spanish explorers and the native chieftain, are only two examples of Tagbilaran's famous Spanish colonial-era architecture. The city's busy port is a popular travel destination for both domestic and foreign travelers, providing access to Bohol's breathtaking natural attractions, such as the renowned Chocolate Hills and the endemic tarsiers. In addition, Tagbilaran City has a variety of eateries, malls, and lodgings, making it a friendly and practical hub for visitors exploring the beauty of Bohol Island.

Governed by




Madam Jane C. Yap exemplifies dedication in her role as mayor, consistently fostering the growth and development of Tagbilaran City. Supported by the dynamic Vice Mayor Hon. Adam Relson L. Jala, the city continues to prosper under their leadership.
